# 2026 Wholesale Cashmere Sweater Pricing: What B2B Buyers Should Expect to Pay (By Country)

As global demand for luxury knitwear continues to rise, B2B buyers sourcing cashmere sweaters face a shifting landscape of raw material costs, labor rates, and trade policies. ## Understanding the 2026 Cashmere Market

Before diving into country-specific prices, it’s essential to grasp the macro trends shaping the 2026 cashmere market. The global cashmere industry is valued at over $3.5 billion, with China accounting for roughly 60-70% of raw cashmere production. Inner Mongolia, particularly Ordos, remains the world’s premier source of high-quality cashmere due to its unique climate and centuries-old herding traditions. However, other countries like Mongolia, Italy, and Scotland have carved out niches in processing and luxury branding.

Key factors influencing 2026 wholesale prices include:
- **Raw material costs**: Cashmere fiber prices have stabilized after post-pandemic volatility, but climate change and grazing regulations in Inner Mongolia are gradually reducing supply. In 2026, raw cashmere is expected to trade at $80–$120 per kilogram for standard grades (14-16 microns), with premium grades (under 14.5 microns) reaching $150–$200/kg.
- **Labor and manufacturing costs**: Rising wages in China (especially in coastal regions) are pushing some production inland to areas like Ordos, where labor is still competitive. Meanwhile, countries like Bangladesh and Vietnam offer lower labor costs but lack cashmere expertise.
- **Tariffs and trade agreements**: Tariff regimes vary widely. For example, the EU imposes a 12% duty on cashmere imports from China, while Mongolia benefits from reduced rates under the EU’s GSP+ scheme. The US has a 6% tariff on cashmere sweaters from China, though Section 301 tariffs may add 7.5% for certain categories.
- **Sustainability certifications**: Buyers increasingly demand OEKO-TEX Standard 100 or GOTS certification, which can add 5-10% to wholesale pricing but are non-negotiable for many European and Japanese markets.

## Country-by-Country Wholesale Cashmere Sweater Pricing in 2026

Below is a detailed breakdown of expected wholesale prices (FOB, per piece) for standard cashmere sweaters (14.5-15.5 micron, 2-ply, 220-250 gsm) in quantities of 500-1,000 units per style. Prices include raw material, manufacturing, and basic quality testing, but exclude shipping, duties, and certifications unless noted.

### 1. China (Mainland, especially Ordos, Inner Mongolia)
- **Price range**: $35–$55 per piece
- **Typical MOQ**: 300-500 pieces per style
- **Lead time**: 45-60 days
- **Strengths**: Unmatched raw material access, vertical integration (from herding to finished garment), skilled labor, and ISO9001-certified factories. Ordos-based factories like DONGXIAO offer full OEM/ODM services with strict quality control.
- **Considerations**: Prices are rising due to environmental regulations in Inner Mongolia and labor shortages in coastal areas. However, for bulk orders, China remains the most cost-effective source for mid-to-premium cashmere. Expect $40–$50 for standard 2-ply sweaters; premium 3-ply or ultra-fine (14 micron) can reach $60–$80.

### 2. Mongolia
- **Price range**: $30–$45 per piece
- **Typical MOQ**: 200-500 pieces per style
- **Lead time**: 60-90 days
- **Strengths**: Lower labor costs and abundant raw cashmere (though often coarser at 16-18 microns). Mongolia benefits from trade preferences with the EU and Japan.
- **Considerations**: Manufacturing infrastructure is less developed than China, with limited capacity for complex knitting or finishing. Quality consistency can be an issue; many Mongolian factories still rely on Chinese yarn for finer gauges. For budget-conscious buyers targeting entry-level luxury, Mongolia offers savings of 15-20% versus China, but be prepared for longer lead times and potential quality variability.

### 3. Italy
- **Price range**: $80–$150 per piece
- **Typical MOQ**: 100-300 pieces per style (often higher for custom runs)
- **Lead time**: 60-120 days
- **Strengths**: Unrivaled craftsmanship, luxury branding, and innovative finishing techniques (e.g., Loro Piana-style treatments). Italian mills often use the finest cashmere (13-14.5 microns) and offer GOTS or RWS certifications.
- **Considerations**: Prices reflect high labor costs, artisan expertise, and small-batch production. For ultra-luxury brands targeting the EU or US high-end market, Italy is the gold standard. However, expect to pay 2-3x more than Chinese equivalents for similar raw material quality.

### 4. Scotland
- **Price range**: $90–$180 per piece
- **Typical MOQ**: 100-200 pieces per style (often bespoke)
- **Lead time**: 90-150 days
- **Strengths**: Heritage brands (e.g., Johnstons of Elgin), premium cashmere from Inner Mongolia or Scotland’s own (limited) supply, and strong sustainability credentials.
- **Considerations**: Scotland’s niche is high-end, sustainably sourced cashmere with traceability. Prices are the highest among major sources, but for buyers seeking “Made in Scotland” prestige, the premium is justified. Expect MOQs to be low but per-unit costs high.

### 5. Bangladesh & Vietnam (Emerging Sources)
- **Price range**: $22–$35 per piece
- **Typical MOQ**: 500-1,000 pieces per style
- **Lead time**: 60-90 days
- **Strengths**: Low labor costs, tariff benefits (e.g., Bangladesh has duty-free access to the EU under Everything But Arms), and growing knitwear expertise.
- **Considerations**: These countries primarily use Chinese or Mongolian cashmere yarn, adding logistics costs and potential quality dilution. They are best suited for budget-friendly cashmere blends (e.g., 70% cashmere, 30% silk or merino) rather than pure cashmere. Quality control and ethical labor practices require careful vetting.

## Hidden Costs Every B2B Buyer Should Budget For

When calculating total landed cost, factor in these often-overlooked expenses:
- **Sample development**: $50–$200 per style for initial samples; revisions cost extra.
- **Testing and certifications**: OEKO-TEX Standard 100 certification adds $500–$2,000 per product line; GOTS certification for organic cashmere can cost $1,000–$5,000 annually.
- **Shipping and insurance**: From China to EU (sea freight), expect $2–$4 per piece for consolidated LCL; air freight can be $8–$15 per piece.
- **Tariffs**: EU 12%, US 6-13.5%, Japan 0-8% depending on origin and trade agreements.
- **Quality control**: Third-party inspections (e.g., SGS, Bureau Veritas) cost $300–$800 per factory visit.
