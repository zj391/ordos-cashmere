# How to Verify Ordos Cashmere Origin: A B2B Buyer’s Guide to Avoid Mislabeling

## Why Ordos Cashmere Origin Matters for B2B Buyers

For global cashmere importers, brand buyers, and trading companies, the term "Ordos cashmere" carries significant weight. Ordos, located in Inner Mongolia, China, is the world’s largest high-quality cashmere production hub, accounting for over 40% of global cashmere output. The region’s unique climate and indigenous cashmere goats produce fibers with exceptional fineness—typically 14–16 microns for Grade A raw cashmere—and a staple length of 34–38 mm, resulting in a soft, durable, and luxurious end product.

However, mislabeling is rampant in the cashmere industry. A 2023 study by the International Cashmere Association found that up to 30% of products labeled as "cashmere" globally contain synthetic blends or inferior fibers. For B2B buyers, sourcing from a verified **Ordos cashmere manufacturer** ensures quality, traceability, and compliance with international standards. This guide provides actionable steps to authenticate Ordos origin and avoid costly mislabeling.

## Understanding the Ordos Cashmere Advantage

### Fiber Characteristics of Ordos Cashmere
- **Micron count**: 14–16 µm (ultrafine), compared to 17–19 µm for lower-grade cashmere from other regions.
- **Staple length**: 34–38 mm, enabling stronger yarns with less pilling.
- **Color**: Naturally white, gray, or brown—white cashmere is most prized for dyeing versatility.
- **Yield**: Ordos goats produce 150–200 grams of dehaired cashmere per animal annually, lower than some other breeds, but with superior fineness.

### Why Mislabeling Occurs
- **Price pressure**: Lower-grade cashmere (e.g., from Mongolia or Iran) or blends with wool, nylon, or acrylic are cheaper.
- **Lack of traceability**: Many factories blend fibers from multiple origins without disclosure.
- **False claims**: Some suppliers label generic Chinese cashmere as "Ordos" to command premium prices.

## Step 1: Verify Supplier Credentials

Before placing an order, conduct due diligence on the **Inner Mongolia cashmere factory** or manufacturer.

### Key Documentation to Request
- **Business license**: Confirm the registered address is in Ordos, Inner Mongolia (e.g., Dongsheng District, Dalad Banner).
- **ISO 9001 certification**: Indicates quality management systems in place (common for reputable factories).
- **OEKO-TEX Standard 100**: Ensures no harmful chemicals in the final product.
- **GOTS certification**: For organic cashmere, if required.
- **Cashmere origin certificate**: Some factories provide a certificate from the local animal husbandry bureau.

### Red Flags
- Supplier refuses to share factory address or audit reports.
- Claims of ultra-low MOQs (e.g., under 50 kg for raw cashmere) without lead times.
- No mention of micron count or staple length in product specs.

## Step 3: Use Physical and Lab Testing

### Simple Physical Tests for B2B Buyers
- **Burn test**: Real cashmere smells like burning hair and forms a crushable ash. Synthetic blends smell like plastic and melt.
- **Fiber feel**: Cashmere is soft, lightweight, and has a slight crimp. Wool feels coarser and prickly.
- **Water absorption**: Cashmere absorbs moisture quickly—drop water on a sample; if it beads up, it may be coated with silicone (common in low-grade blends).

### Professional Lab Testing
- **Fiber composition analysis**: Use ISO 1833 or AATCC 20A to detect wool, nylon, or acrylic blends. Acceptable purity is >95% cashmere for Grade A.
- **Micron measurement**: Use laser-based instruments (e.g., OFDA) to confirm 14–16 µm for Ordos cashmere.
- **DNA testing**: Advanced labs can identify goat species (Capra hircus) and even geographic origin via isotope analysis.

### Cost and Time
- Basic fiber test: $50–150 per sample, 3–5 business days.
- Full origin DNA test: $300–500, 10–14 days.

### Mislabeling Examples

| "Hand-combed cashmere" | Machine-combed with chemical softening | Check for silicone residue (burn test) |
| "Inner Mongolia origin" | Sourced from Hebei or Shandong | Audit factory location and supply chain |

## Step 6: Build Long-Term Supplier Relationships

### Best Practices for B2B Cashmere Sourcing
1. **Audit factories in person**: Visit Ordos during the shearing season (April–June) to see raw cashmere collection.
2. **Request samples**: Test 3–5 batches from different production runs before bulk orders.
