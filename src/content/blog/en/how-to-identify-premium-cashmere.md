# How to Identify Premium Cashmere: 6 Key Metrics

When you source cashmere for a fashion brand or textile mill, the difference between commodity fiber and premium fiber can mean 30–50% price gap on the finished garment. Use this checklist before signing any purchase order.

## 1. Fineness (Micron Count)

The single most important number. Measured in micrometers (μm).

- **Baby Cashmere**: ≤ 14.5 μm — top of the line, harvested from yearling goats
- **Premium White**: 14.5–15.5 μm — Albas white goat from Ordos, Inner Mongolia
- **Standard White**: 15.5–16.5 μm — most commercial cashmere
- **Brown / Purple**: 15.5–16.5 μm — natural color, lower price point

Anything above 16.5 μm is no longer classified as cashmere by international trade standards.

## 2. Fiber Length

Longer fibers spin into stronger, less pilling yarn.

- **Premium**: 34–42 mm
- **Standard**: 28–34 mm
- **Short fiber (<28 mm)**: produces weaker yarn, more shedding

## 3. Dehaired Yield

The percentage of usable fiber after dehairing raw cashmere.

- **Premium**: ≥ 45%
- **Standard**: 35–45%
- **Low (<35%)**: indicates poor raw material or excessive processing

## 4. Color & Whiteness

Premium white cashmere (Albas goat) has natural high whiteness — can be dyed into any pastel color with minimal bleaching. Brown and purple cashmere have natural dark tones, ideal for blends and dark colors.

## 5. Impurity Content

- **Premium**: ≤ 0.3% (vegetable matter, grease, dirt)
- **Standard**: 0.3–0.5%
- Above 0.5% means additional processing costs and lower yields

## 6. Crimp & Handle

Premium cashmere has tight, regular crimp and a soft "handle" (touch feel). Run a fiber through your fingers — premium fibers feel slippery, not rough.

## How to Verify Before Ordering

1. Request a **lab test report** (fiber diameter, length, yield) from a SGS or Intertek lab
2. Ask for **batch samples** before placing bulk orders
3. Visit the factory or hire a third-party inspector (Asia Inspection, Bureau Veritas)
