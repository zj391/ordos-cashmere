## Common Mislabeling Tactics in Cashmere Trade

Mislabeling can range from exaggerating fiber purity to falsely claiming Ordos origin. Common issues include:
- **Fiber blending**: Adding cheaper fibers (e.g., wool, nylon) without disclosure.
- **Origin fraud**: Labeling cashmere as “Inner Mongolian” or “Ordos” when sourced from other regions like Iran or Afghanistan.
- **Fake certifications**: Using counterfeit documents to mislead buyers.

According to industry reports, up to **30%** of cashmere products sold globally may be mislabeled in some form. For B2B importers, this poses legal risks, brand damage, and customer dissatisfaction.

## How to Verify Ordos Origin: A Step-by-Step Guide for B2B Buyers

### 1. Request Third-Party Lab Testing

Laboratory analysis is the most reliable method to confirm fiber composition and origin. Key tests include:
- **Microscopic analysis**: Differentiates cashmere from wool or synthetic fibers.
- **DNA testing**: Can trace fiber origin to specific goat breeds (e.g., Inner Mongolian cashmere goats).
- **Isotope analysis**: Matches fiber chemistry to regional soil and water profiles.

**Recommended labs**: SGS, Bureau Veritas, or Intertek offer specialized cashmere testing. Ensure your supplier provides test reports for every batch.

### 2. Audit Supplier Certifications

A reputable **Inner Mongolia cashmere factory** will hold certifications that prove ethical and quality standards. Look for:
- **ISO 9001**: Quality management systems.
- **OEKO-TEX Standard 100**: No harmful substances.
- **GOTS**: Organic fiber processing (if applicable).
- **SCS Global Services**: Origin verification.

Always verify certificates directly with the issuing body. Fraudulent suppliers may use expired or fake documents.

### 3. Visit the Factory or Use Remote Audits

On-site visits are ideal, but remote audits via video calls or third-party inspectors can also work. During an audit, check:
- **Raw material storage**: Look for distinct labeling of Inner Mongolian cashmere.
- **Production records**: Traceability logs from herder to finished product.
- **Employee knowledge**: Workers should understand origin claims.

### 4. Request Traceability Documentation

A transparent **Ordos cashmere manufacturer** will provide:
- **Purchase contracts** from local herder cooperatives.
- **Transportation records** showing origin from Ordos region.
- **Batch numbers** linking raw material to finished goods.

### 5. Check Supplier Reputation

Research the factory’s history in B2B networks like Alibaba, Global Sources, or Made-in-China. Look for:
- **Years in business**: DONGXIAO has been operating for 23 years.
- **Client testimonials**: Feedback from other importers.
